Ohio University ACM
Who We Are
The Association of Computing Machinery is an international organization for advancing technology fields. Here at Ohio University, our local chapter is designed to help advance ACM's Mission and help students grow. We are a group of students who are passionate about technology and helping each other learn more than what is taught in classes.
What We Do
OU ACM hosts a variety of events each semester. We have weekly meetings where we have had guest presentations, programming or job search workshops, and social events. In addition, our members are encouraged to participate in various additional activities such as hackathons, cybersecurity Capture The Flag (CTF) events, and programming competitions.

ICPC
"The International Collegiate Programming Contest is an algorithmic programming contest for college students. Teams of three, representing their university, work to solve the most real-world problems, fostering collaboration, creativity, innovation, and the ability to perform under pressure. Through training and competition, teams challenge each other to raise the bar on the possible. Quite simply, it is the oldest, largest, and most prestigious programming contest in the world." - ICPC
Blossom Hack
"The first and best hackathon hosted at Ohio University by OUGDA, NSBE, SWE, IEEE, and ACM. This event is open to students of all backgrounds. Our mission is to provide students with technical and professional opportunities outside of the confines of the classroom." - Blossom Hack
